Comparing deep learning stroke segmentation in NCCT, CTA, and CTP: Accuracy, domain transfer, and temporal sampling effect. *Medical Physics*. [41933279](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41933279/)

**Research Question:** Which CT imaging modality yields the most accurate deep learning–based stroke core segmentation, and how well do these models transfer across clinical sites?

Automated stroke core segmentation improves substantially with more advanced CT acquisitions — Dice score rising from 0.36 with NCCT to 0.78 with CTP — but cross-site deployment requires fine-tuning regardless of modality.

**Details:**
- Design: Comparative deep learning study (nnU-Net), N=91 stroke patients (primary site) + 166 cases (second site for transfer learning), single-centre training with cross-site validation
- Primary outcome: Modified Dice coefficient for stroke core segmentation in 10–70 mL infarcts: NCCT 0.36 ± 0.28, CTA 0.55 ± 0.27, mCTA 0.71 ± 0.22, CTP 0.78 ± 0.09; fine-tuning consistently improved cross-site performance across all modalities
- Limitations: Small training dataset (91 cases at primary site) and retrospective single-centre training limit generalisability; no clinical outcome validation to confirm segmentation accuracy translates to treatment benefit
