**Rehabilitation**

Home based, tailored intervention to reduce rate of falls after stroke (FAST): randomised trial. *BMJ*. [41876122](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41876122/)

**Research Question:** Does a multidisciplinary, home-based intervention combining functional exercise, fall hazard reduction, and community mobility coaching reduce falls in community-dwelling stroke survivors?

A 6-month tailored physiotherapy and occupational therapy intervention reduced falls by 33% over 12 months in ambulatory stroke survivors — clinicians should consider this model for patients discharged to the community who remain at fall risk.

**Details:**
- Design: Randomised controlled trial, N=370, community-dwelling adults >50 years within 5 years of stroke, able to walk 10 m, across 3 Australian states
- Primary outcome: Falls rate at 12 months — IRR 0.67 (95% CI 0.48–0.94; P=0.02), a 33% reduction; no significant difference in proportion of fallers (ARR 0.03, 95% CI −0.07 to 0.13; P=0.52)
- Limitations: Reduction in fall rate without a significant reduction in the proportion of fallers suggests the benefit may be concentrated in repeat fallers; generalisability is limited by exclusion of moderate-to-severe receptive aphasia and fast walkers without recent falls

**Rehabilitation**

Robot-Assisted Therapy for Upper Limb Rehabilitation After Stroke: Umbrella Review. *Journal of Medical Internet Research*. [41879816](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41879816/)

**Research Question:** Does robot-assisted therapy improve upper limb function and activities of daily living after stroke compared to conventional therapy?

Robot-assisted therapy improves upper limb motor function across stroke stages, but does not significantly outperform conventional therapy for activities of daily living — meaning functional gains at the body level are not reliably translating into real-world independence.

**Details:**
- Design: Umbrella review of 21 meta-analyses, 535 RCTs, N=27,598, across acute, subacute, and chronic stroke stages; AMSTAR 2 quality assessment (17 high quality, 3 moderate, 1 critically low)
- Primary outcome: RAT superior for upper limb motor function; no statistically significant advantage in activities of daily living vs. conventional therapy; treatment effects modified by stroke stage, impairment severity, and robot type
- Limitations: Umbrella reviews inherit the heterogeneity and methodological limitations of included meta-analyses; outcome measures varied substantially across studies, limiting comparability; long-term maintenance of gains is not established

**Acute Treatment**

Effectiveness and safety of endovascular therapy compared to intravenous thrombolysis in acute ischaemic stroke due to medium-vessel occlusions: a real-world multicentre study from the Italian SITS registry. *European Stroke Journal*. [41904677](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41904677/)

**Research Question:** Does endovascular therapy improve 90-day functional outcomes compared to intravenous thrombolysis alone in patients with medium-vessel occlusion stroke in routine clinical practice?

EVT offered no functional benefit over IVT alone in MeVO stroke (mRS 0–2: 60.6% vs 60.9%; OR 0.99) but increased intracranial haemorrhage risk, reinforcing a conservative approach to EVT in this population.

**Details:**
- Design: Retrospective multicentre registry study (SITS registry, 82 Italian centres, 2020–2023); propensity score–matched cohort, N=780 (390 per group)
- Primary outcome: Functional independence (mRS 0–2) at 90 days — 60.6% EVT±IVT vs 60.9% IVT alone (OR 0.99; 95% CI 0.73–1.34; P=.939)
- Limitations: Retrospective registry design with incomplete procedural data and anatomical heterogeneity in MeVO classification limits causal inference and generalisability

**Acute Treatment**

Functional Outcome Following Blood Pressure Drops Within 24 Hours After Successful Mechanical Thrombectomy. *Neurology*. [41886712](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41886712/)

**Research Question:** Does systolic blood pressure instability — specifically drops ≥40 mmHg within one hour — during the first 24 hours after successful thrombectomy worsen functional outcomes at 3 months?

Post-thrombectomy BP drops occurred in 41% of patients and were independently associated with worse 3-month disability (adjusted OR 1.66) and more than double the rate of early neurologic deterioration, suggesting that hemodynamic vigilance after reperfusion deserves the same attention as during the procedure.

**Details:**
- Design: Retrospective multicenter observational study, N=300, acute ischemic stroke patients with successful recanalization (mTICI ≥2b) from 2 German tertiary stroke centers, 2020–2022
- Primary outcome: mRS shift analysis at 3 months — any BP drop associated with worse disability (adjusted common OR 1.66, 95% CI 1.02–2.70); each additional drop independently associated with worse outcome (adjusted common OR 1.33 per drop, 95% CI 1.05–1.69); early neurologic deterioration 22.2% vs 13.4% (adjusted OR 2.22, 95% CI 1.03–4.78)
- Limitations: Retrospective design with data from only 2 centers limits generalizability, and the observational nature prevents distinguishing whether BP drops cause worse outcomes or reflect sicker patients with more severe strokes

**Rehabilitation**

Effect of noninvasive brain stimulation for unilateral spatial neglect after stroke: a systematic review and meta-analysis. *Journal of Neurology*. [41880010](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41880010/)

**Research Question:** Does noninvasive brain stimulation (NIBS) improve unilateral spatial neglect after stroke, and which modality and protocol configuration are most effective?

rTMS and TBS significantly reduced neglect severity across multiple standardized assessments, with effect sizes exceeding those of tDCS, while tACS showed no benefit — and early intervention with excitatory protocols targeting the ipsilesional posterior parietal cortex appeared to maximize treatment response.

**Details:**
- Design: Systematic review and meta-analysis of 17 RCTs or crossover RCTs, N = 425 patients with post-stroke unilateral spatial neglect
- Primary outcome: rTMS reduced line bisection test (SMD = −1.82, P < 0.00001), star cancellation test (SMD = −1.59, P < 0.00001), and Catherine Bergego Scale (MD = −2.76, P = 0.002); TBS reduced SCT (SMD = −2.59, P = 0.03) and CBS (MD = −0.93, P < 0.0001); tDCS showed smaller but significant effects on LBT (SMD = −0.60) and SCT (SMD = −0.60); tACS showed no significant effect
- Limitations: Only 17 studies with 425 total patients; substantial heterogeneity in protocols, stroke phase, and lesion location limits the ability to draw firm conclusions about optimal parameters

No-reflow phenomenon after vessel recanalization in acute ischemic stroke: mechanisms, diagnosis, and treatment directions. *Journal of Neurology*. [41915244](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41915244/)

Persistent microvascular non-perfusion after successful recanalization affects 3.3–63% of AIS patients and represents a critical, underrecognized barrier to good outcomes that current reperfusion strategies do not reliably address.

**Details:**
- Design: Narrative review; N not applicable; population: AIS patients undergoing reperfusion therapy
- Primary outcome: Prevalence of no-reflow phenomenon ranges from 3.3% to 63% after reperfusion therapy; driven by distal embolization, microthrombus formation, inflammatory responses, cerebral edema, and neurovascular unit dysfunction

**Imaging**

Comparative Accuracy of TCD, TTE, TEE, and Cardiac CT in Detecting Right-to-Left Shunt in Embolic Stroke of Undetermined Source. *Neurology: Clinical Practice*. [41908736](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41908736/)

**Research Question:** Which imaging modality — TCD, TTE, TEE, or cardiac CT — performs best for detecting right-to-left shunt in patients with embolic stroke of undetermined source?

TCD offers 100% sensitivity and NPV for RLS detection, making it the strongest first-line rule-out tool, while TEE provides the best overall accuracy (91.3%) and cardiac CT, despite perfect specificity, misses more than half of confirmed PFOs.

**Details:**
- Design: Retrospective single-center comparative study, N=130 ESUS patients who underwent ≥3 of 4 modalities
- Primary outcome: TCD sensitivity 100%, specificity 75%, accuracy 87.6%; TEE sensitivity 95.1%, specificity 87.5%, accuracy 91.3%; TTE sensitivity 83.3%, specificity 93.3%, accuracy 88.1%; cardiac CT sensitivity 45.8%, specificity 100% — AUC significantly lower for CT vs. all three others (p<0.05), no significant differences among TCD, TTE, and TEE
- Limitations: Class IV evidence only; single-center retrospective design with referral and selection bias limits generalizability

**Imaging**

Automated Intracranial Hemorrhage Detection: Real-World Experience in a Large Comprehensive Stroke Center. *Neurology: Clinical Practice*. [41908735](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41908735/)

**Research Question:** How does automated ICH detection software perform compared with board-certified radiologists in real-world code stroke practice?

Automated ICH detection achieved 81% overall sensitivity and 99% specificity, but sensitivity for isolated extra-axial hemorrhage was only 43% — a critical blind spot that clinicians must not overlook when relying on these tools.

**Details:**
- Design: Retrospective cohort, N=1,402 code stroke noncontrast CT scans, single comprehensive stroke center, January 2022–February 2023
- Primary outcome: Sensitivity 81% (95% CI 74%–88%), specificity 99% (98%–99%), PPV 85%, NPV 98%; sensitivity was 94% for intra-axial hemorrhage but only 43% for isolated extra-axial hemorrhage
- Limitations: Single-center retrospective design limits generalizability; 32 scans (2%) excluded for motion artifact, and radiologist reports used as gold standard rather than independent adjudication

**Prevention**

Discrepant Outcomes of Direct Oral Anticoagulant Trials in Device-Detected Atrial Fibrillation after Stroke: Insights from NOAH-AFNET 6 and ARTESIA. *Clinical and Applied Thrombosis/Hemostasis*. [41877615](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41877615/)

**Research Question:** Should patients with device-detected atrial fibrillation and prior stroke or TIA receive direct oral anticoagulants for secondary stroke prevention?

Across NOAH-AFNET 6 and ARTESIA, DOACs reduced ischemic stroke in this population but at the cost of increased major bleeding, with discordant benefit-risk signals between trials leaving the net clinical benefit unresolved.

**Details:**
- Design: Narrative review synthesizing two RCTs (NOAH-AFNET 6 and ARTESIA); specific N not reported in abstract
- Primary outcome: In ARTESIA, apixaban reduced ischemic stroke with a net coherent benefit-risk signal in the prior stroke/TIA subgroup; in NOAH-AFNET 6, ischemic stroke reduction was modest and non-significant while major bleeding increased substantially
- Limitations: Subgroup-level data from two trials with discordant results limits generalizability; this is a review article, not primary data, and patient-level pooled analysis is absent

Transforming Stroke Diagnosis with Artificial Intelligence: A Scoping Review of Brainomix e-Stroke, Aidoc, RapidAI, and Viz.ai. *Medicina (Kaunas, Lithuania)*. [41901663](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41901663/)

**Research Question:** How do the four leading AI stroke platforms compare in diagnostic accuracy, workflow impact, and cost-effectiveness across real-world clinical settings?

All four platforms show high LVO sensitivity (78–97%) and meaningful workflow gains, but their outputs are not interchangeable and none has demonstrated definitive superiority — institutional selection should be driven by local validation and specific workflow priorities.

**Details:**
- Design: Scoping review (PRISMA-ScR), N = 29 studies, 2019–2025, peer-reviewed and HTA sources
- Primary outcome: LVO sensitivity 78–97%; Viz.ai associated with door-to-puncture reductions of 11–25 minutes; volumetric discrepancies identified between RapidAI and Viz.ai perfusion outputs; Brainomix validated for automated NCCT ASPECTS triage
- Limitations: Scoping review design precludes meta-analytic pooling; included studies vary substantially in methodology and reflect manufacturer-influenced evidence clusters rather than head-to-head comparative trials
