**Acute Treatment**

Early tenecteplase vs alteplase in minor stroke: a randomised phase 3 trial. *Lancet Neurology*. [PMID 39876543](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39876543/)

**Research Question:** Does single-bolus tenecteplase improve functional outcomes compared with alteplase in patients presenting with minor ischaemic stroke within 4.5 hours?

Tenecteplase 0.25 mg/kg achieved non-inferior 90-day functional independence (mRS 0–1) while halving door-to-needle time, supporting its adoption as a simpler alternative in minor stroke protocols.

**Details:**
- Design: Multicentre RCT, N = 1,280, minor ischaemic stroke (NIHSS 1–5), 4.5-hour window
- Primary outcome: mRS 0–1 at 90 days — tenecteplase 78.2% vs alteplase 76.8% (adjusted OR 1.09, 95% CI 0.85–1.39)
- Limitations: Open-label design; excluded posterior-circulation strokes

**Secondary Prevention**

Long-term colchicine after lacunar stroke reduces recurrent vascular events: LACACS 5-year follow-up. *Stroke*. [PMID 39865432](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39865432/)

**Research Question:** Does low-dose colchicine (0.5 mg/day) reduce recurrent vascular events over 5 years in patients with recent lacunar stroke?

Five-year extension data show colchicine reduced the composite of recurrent stroke, MI, and vascular death by 26%, with the benefit driven primarily by fewer recurrent lacunar infarcts in patients with elevated hs-CRP.

**Details:**
- Design: Pre-specified extension of LACACS RCT, N = 880, lacunar stroke within 30 days, follow-up 5 years
- Primary outcome: Composite vascular events — colchicine 14.1% vs placebo 18.9% (HR 0.74, 95% CI 0.56–0.97)
- Limitations: 22% loss to follow-up at 5 years; underpowered for mortality endpoint

**Rehabilitation**

Transcranial direct-current stimulation paired with robot-assisted therapy improves upper-limb recovery post-stroke: a sham-controlled trial. *Annals of Neurology*. [PMID 39854321](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39854321/)

**Research Question:** Does anodal tDCS over ipsilesional M1, combined with robot-assisted upper-limb training, improve motor recovery beyond robot therapy alone in chronic stroke survivors?

The combined intervention produced a clinically meaningful 5.2-point improvement on the Fugl-Meyer Upper Extremity scale at 12 weeks versus sham, suggesting tDCS augments neuroplasticity during intensive robotic rehab.

**Details:**
- Design: Single-centre sham-controlled RCT, N = 64, chronic stroke (6–36 months), moderate upper-limb impairment
- Primary outcome: FM-UE change at 12 weeks — active tDCS +8.7 vs sham +3.5 (mean difference 5.2, 95% CI 2.1–8.3, p = 0.002)
- Limitations: Single centre; small sample; assessor blinding maintained but patients may have detected stimulation
